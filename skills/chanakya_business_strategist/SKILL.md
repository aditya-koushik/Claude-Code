# Chanakya Business Strategist

## Purpose
Apply Chanakya's Arthashastra principles to modern business strategy, competitive positioning, and alliance building. This skill brings ancient strategic wisdom to enterprise decision-making: assessing power dynamics, mapping adversaries and allies, and choosing the right strategic approach (Sam-Dam-Dand-Bhed) for each situation. Used by Vidur and Rama when navigating competitive landscapes, partnerships, and market entry battles.

## When to Use
- Entering a market with entrenched competitors
- Evaluating partnership or alliance opportunities
- Planning competitive response to an aggressive rival
- Navigating complex stakeholder dynamics (investors, partners, regulators)
- Deciding between cooperation and confrontation strategies

## Workflow

### Step 1: Assess Current Position
Map your own strengths, weaknesses, resources, and vulnerabilities honestly. Identify your "treasury" (cash, talent, IP, audience) and your "army" (team capabilities, distribution channels, technology stack).

### Step 2: Identify Allies and Adversaries
Categorize all players in the landscape: direct competitors, potential allies, neutral parties, and kingmakers. Assess each player's strength, motivation, and reliability. Determine who can be won over and who must be outmaneuvered.

### Step 3: Map the Competitive Landscape
Chart power dynamics: who controls what resources, where are the dependencies, what alliances already exist, and where are the fault lines. Identify the "mandala" (circle of influence) around your position.

### Step 4: Select Strategic Approach (Sam-Dam-Dand-Bhed)
Choose the appropriate strategy for each relationship:
- **Sam (Persuasion)**: Negotiate, align interests, demonstrate mutual benefit
- **Dam (Incentive)**: Offer value, create economic alignment, share resources
- **Dand (Force)**: Compete aggressively, outspend, outship, outmarket
- **Bhed (Division)**: Exploit competitor weaknesses, recruit their talent, fragment their alliances

### Step 5: Create Action Plan
Produce a sequenced action plan with specific moves, timelines, contingencies, and success metrics. Include fallback positions for each major move.

## Output Format
- **Position Assessment**: Current strength rating (1-10) across key dimensions
- **Stakeholder Map**: Allies, adversaries, and neutrals with influence scores
- **Strategy Selection**: Chosen approach per relationship with rationale
- **Action Plan**: Sequenced moves with timelines and contingencies
- **Intelligence Gaps**: What you need to learn before executing

## Key Principles
- Know your strengths completely before attacking anyone else's weaknesses
- Build alliances before conflicts arise; the best war is the one never fought
- Information is the ultimate weapon; invest in intelligence before action
- Patience is strategy; premature moves waste resources and reveal intent
- A wise king makes his enemy's strength into a weakness
- Never fight on two fronts simultaneously
