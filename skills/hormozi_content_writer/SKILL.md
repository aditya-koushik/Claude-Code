# Hormozi Content Writer

## Purpose
Creates persuasive content using Alex Hormozi's value equation. Every piece must make the offer so clear and valuable that saying no feels like a bad decision. Leads with specific outcomes, stacks proof, and removes all perceived risk.

## When to Use
- When writing content designed to drive conversions (signups, demos, purchases)
- When positioning an offer or product
- When creating landing page copy or sales content
- When a post needs to move readers from interest to action

## Workflow

### Step 1: Define the Dream Outcome
State the exact result your audience wants. Not features. Outcomes. "Save 20 hours per week on reporting" not "automated reporting tool." Be specific. Use their words, not yours.

### Step 2: Increase Perceived Likelihood
Stack proof that this outcome is achievable. Use customer stories, specific numbers, case studies, before/after comparisons. "347 companies already use this" beats "many companies trust us."

### Step 3: Minimize Time Delay
Show how fast results happen. "See results in 14 days, not 14 months." If setup takes 2 hours, say it. Short time-to-value wins every time.

### Step 4: Minimize Effort and Sacrifice
Show how easy it is. What do they NOT have to do? "No code required. No training needed. Works with your existing tools." Remove every friction point you can.

### Step 5: Stack Proof and Social Proof
Layer multiple types of proof: testimonials, case studies, data, logos, awards. One proof point is an anecdote. Three proof points is a pattern. Five is undeniable.

### Step 6: Create Urgency Without Fakeness
Use real scarcity or real deadlines. Never fake urgency. "We onboard 5 companies per month" is real. "ONLY 3 SPOTS LEFT!!!" is fake. Readers can tell the difference.

### Step 7: Clear CTA
One action. One link. One next step. Do not give three options. Tell them exactly what to do next.

## Output Format
Content following the value equation structure: Dream Outcome, Proof of Likelihood, Speed, Ease, Social Proof, Urgency, CTA. Each element should flow as natural prose, not a formula checklist.

## Key Principles
- Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort/Sacrifice)
- Lead with outcomes, not features. Nobody buys a drill. They buy a hole.
- Use real numbers. "37% faster" beats "significantly faster."
- Make the offer so good people feel stupid saying no.
- Stack bonuses. Remove risk with guarantees.
- Mental models: Value Density, First Principles, Enterprise DNA.
