# Proof Stacking Examples

## What Is Proof Stacking?

One proof point is an anecdote. Three is a pattern. Five is undeniable. Stack different types of proof to make your claim bulletproof. Each type of proof addresses a different objection in the buyer's mind.

---

## The 6 Types of Proof

| Type | What It Proves | Best For |
|------|---------------|----------|
| Customer Results | "This works for people like me" | Overcoming skepticism |
| Data Points | "The numbers back this up" | Analytical buyers |
| Case Studies | "I can see the full story" | Complex B2B sales |
| Logos/Clients | "Respected companies trust this" | Enterprise buyers |
| Awards/Certifications | "Third parties validate this" | Risk-averse buyers |
| Testimonials | "Real humans vouch for this" | Building emotional trust |

## Example 1: AI Document Processing SaaS

**Claim:** "Our AI processes invoices 10x faster than manual entry with zero errors."

**Proof Stack:**

1. **Customer Result:** "Meridian Logistics cut invoice processing from 14 days to 3 days. Their AP team now closes books by the 5th of every month."

2. **Data Point:** "Across 347 customers, average processing time dropped 82%. Error rate went from 4.7% to 0.02%."

3. **Case Study:** "BrightPath Manufacturing was drowning in 12,000 invoices per month. Two people worked full-time on data entry. After implementation, those two employees moved to vendor negotiations. BrightPath saved $340,000 in year one. Not from the software. From redeploying their people."

4. **Logos:** "Used by Deloitte, Accenture, KPMG, and 40+ mid-market companies across manufacturing, logistics, and financial services."

5. **Award:** "SOC 2 Type II certified. Named to CFO Magazine's 'Top 10 Finance Tools of 2026.'"

**Why This Stack Works for B2B Enterprise:**
Enterprise buyers have three fears: it will not work (customer result kills this), it is too risky (certification kills this), and their peers are not using it (logos kill this). This stack addresses all three in sequence.

## Proof Type Selection Guide for B2B Enterprise

| Buyer Objection | Best Proof Type | Why |
|----------------|----------------|-----|
| "This will not work for us" | Case study from their industry | Shows the mechanism, not just the result |
| "This is too risky" | Certifications + logos | Third-party validation removes perceived risk |
| "My boss will not approve this" | ROI data + customer results | Gives them the business case to present internally |
| "We tried something similar and it failed" | Testimonial from a skeptic | "I was skeptical too" is the most powerful opening |
| "Our situation is unique" | Multiple case studies across verticals | Proves the tool works in varied contexts |
| "I do not trust the numbers" | Third-party awards + independent reviews | Removes the "they made these numbers up" objection |

## Rules for Proof Stacking

1. Always lead with the proof type that kills the strongest objection.
2. Use at least 3 different types. Same-type proof gets diminishing returns.
3. Every number must be specific. "Over 300 customers" is weaker than "347 customers."
4. Name real companies when possible. "A Fortune 500 client" is half as credible as "Deloitte."
5. Order from most credible to most emotional. Data first, testimonial last.
