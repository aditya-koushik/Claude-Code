# CTA Patterns

## 10 High-Converting CTA Patterns for B2B Enterprise

Each pattern includes the structure, an example, when to use it, and why it works.

---

### Pattern 1: The Specific Demo

**Structure:** Book a [TIME]-minute [MEETING TYPE]. We will [SPECIFIC ACTION] live.

**Examples:**
- "Book a 15-minute demo. We will process 50 of your real invoices live."
- "Book a 20-minute walkthrough. We will show you exactly how Meridian cut processing time by 82%."
- "Schedule a 15-minute screen share. We will build your first automated workflow in real time."

**When to use:** Product-led offers where seeing is believing. Best when you can show the product doing something impressive in a short window.

**Why it works:** Specificity removes fear. "15 minutes" signals you respect their time. "Your real invoices" proves confidence in the product. The buyer knows exactly what will happen.

---

### Pattern 2: The Peer Result

**Structure:** See how [COMPANY] [ACHIEVED SPECIFIC RESULT]. [TIME] [MEETING TYPE].

**Examples:**
- "See how Desert Sun Realty tripled revenue in 11 months. 20-minute case study walkthrough."
- "See how StackPay cut incident detection from 23 minutes to 47 seconds. 15-minute breakdown."
- "See how Gong produces 72 content variations per campaign with a 5-person team. Quick call."

**When to use:** When you have a strong case study from a company the buyer respects or relates to. Best for enterprise buyers who need to see proof before they engage.

**Why it works:** The buyer is not clicking to hear your pitch. They are clicking to learn how a peer company solved a problem they also have. The sale happens during the story.

---

### Pattern 3: The Free Pilot

**Structure:** Start your free [DURATION] pilot. [SPECIFIC SCOPE]. No [COMMON OBJECTION].

**Examples:**
- "Start your free 14-day pilot. Monitor up to 10 million API calls. No credit card required."
- "Start your free 30-day pilot. Automate your first 5 workflows. No developer time needed."
- "Try it free for 2 weeks. Process up to 500 invoices. Cancel anytime."

**When to use:** Product-led growth plays where the product sells itself once people use it. Best when the free tier is generous enough to prove value but limited enough to drive conversion.

**Why it works:** "Free" removes all financial risk. The specific scope ("10 million API calls") shows confidence. The objection killer ("no credit card") removes the last friction point.

---

### Pattern 4: The ROI Calculator

**Structure:** Calculate your [METRIC]. [QUICK ACTION] using our [TOOL].

**Examples:**
- "Calculate your content ROI. Plug in your team size and output goals. Takes 2 minutes."
- "See what automation saves you. Enter your workflow count and average processing time. Instant results."
- "Find out how much manual invoice processing costs you. Free ROI calculator. 60 seconds."

**When to use:** When the buyer does not know they have a problem, or underestimates the cost of inaction. Best for expensive products where the ROI math needs to be obvious.

**Why it works:** The buyer does their own math. Self-generated numbers are 10x more convincing than numbers you hand them. They sell themselves.

---

### Pattern 5: The Audit/Assessment

**Structure:** Get your free [SPECIFIC] audit. We will [DELIVERABLE] in [TIME].

**Examples:**
- "Get your free API health audit. We will map every endpoint and flag the top 5 risks. Results in 48 hours."
- "Get a free content operations assessment. We will identify your 3 biggest bottlenecks. 30-minute session."
- "Request your free workflow audit. We will find the 10 processes costing you the most time. Delivered in 3 days."

**When to use:** Consulting-heavy or high-ACV products where the buyer needs a trusted advisor, not a product demo. Best for complex enterprise sales with long decision cycles.

**Why it works:** You give value before asking for money. The deliverable is specific and useful whether or not they buy. It positions you as the expert who found the problem, making you the natural choice to fix it.

---

### Pattern 6: The Limited Capacity

**Structure:** We onboard [NUMBER] [CLIENTS] per [PERIOD]. [SPECIFIC NEXT STEP].

**Examples:**
- "We onboard 5 new agencies per month. Book your discovery call before March closes."
- "We take on 3 new enterprise clients per quarter. Spots for Q2 open now."
- "We run 10 pilots per month. 4 are already claimed for April. Grab yours."

**When to use:** When you genuinely have capacity constraints. Only use real scarcity. Buyers smell fake urgency instantly and it destroys trust.

**Why it works:** Real scarcity creates real urgency. "5 per month" is believable. "ONLY 2 SPOTS LEFT!!!" is not. The specific number proves the constraint is operational, not manufactured.

---

### Pattern 7: The Quick Win

**Structure:** [ACHIEVE RESULT] in [SHORT TIME]. [SIMPLE FIRST STEP].

**Examples:**
- "Automate your first workflow in 3 hours. Start with our guided setup."
- "Process your first 100 invoices in under 2 hours. One-click install."
- "Get your first AI-scored leads by tomorrow. Connect your CRM in 10 minutes."

**When to use:** When speed-to-value is your competitive advantage. Best for products that deliver a visible result fast. The quicker the win, the stronger the CTA.

**Why it works:** Short time frames feel low-risk. "3 hours" is a lunch break experiment, not a quarterly initiative. The buyer can try without committing.

---

### Pattern 8: The Comparison

**Structure:** Compare [YOUR APPROACH] vs [THEIR CURRENT APPROACH]. [ACTION].

**Examples:**
- "See how AI lead scoring compares to your current process. Side-by-side analysis. 15 minutes."
- "Compare: 6 months of custom development vs 2 hours of setup. See the breakdown."
- "Your current invoice process costs $X per month. Ours costs $Y. See the math."

**When to use:** When the buyer is comparing options or stuck with the status quo. Best for replacing an existing solution or manual process where the gap is dramatic.

**Why it works:** The buyer already knows their current pain. Showing the contrast makes the decision obvious. You are not selling. You are presenting math.

---

### Pattern 9: The Community/Content

**Structure:** Join [NUMBER] [PEOPLE] who [GET VALUE]. [LOW-COMMITMENT ACTION].

**Examples:**
- "Join 9,400 operators who get AI strategy breakdowns every Tuesday. Subscribe free."
- "Join 800 founders in the Bit Foundry newsletter. Zero fluff. Pure tactics. Free."
- "Follow for daily breakdowns of AI tools that actually work for enterprises."

**When to use:** Top-of-funnel content where the reader is not ready to buy. Best for building an audience and nurturing leads over time. Use when asking for a demo would be premature.

**Why it works:** Low commitment. High perceived value. The number provides social proof. The specific cadence ("every Tuesday") sets expectations. The reader gets value before you ever ask for money.

---

### Pattern 10: The Guarantee Reversal

**Structure:** [PROMISE]. If [CONDITION], [GUARANTEE]. [ACTION].

**Examples:**
- "Hit 99.99% uptime in 60 days. If you do not, we refund every dollar. Start your pilot."
- "Increase lead conversion by 2x in 90 days. If we miss the target, you pay nothing. Book a call."
- "Cut invoice processing time by 50% in 30 days. If we fail, the pilot is free. Get started."

**When to use:** When the buyer's biggest objection is risk. Best for high-ticket offers where the buyer needs to justify the spend internally. The guarantee makes the decision feel reversible.

**Why it works:** You transfer all risk from the buyer to yourself. This signals extreme confidence. The specific conditions ("99.99% in 60 days") prove you have done this before. The buyer's internal pitch becomes: "If it does not work, it costs us nothing."

## CTA Selection Guide

| Content Goal | Best CTA Patterns | Why |
|-------------|-------------------|-----|
| Drive demo signups | #1 Specific Demo, #2 Peer Result | Direct path to sales conversation |
| Generate trials | #3 Free Pilot, #7 Quick Win | Low friction, product does the selling |
| Build pipeline | #5 Audit/Assessment, #4 ROI Calculator | Value-first, positions you as expert |
| Create urgency | #6 Limited Capacity, #10 Guarantee Reversal | Real scarcity or risk reversal |
| Grow audience | #9 Community/Content | Low commitment, high volume |
| Displace competitor | #8 Comparison, #10 Guarantee Reversal | Math and confidence beat features |

## Rules for CTAs

1. One CTA per piece. Not two. Not three. One.
2. Tell them exactly what happens next. "Book a demo" is vague. "Book a 15-minute demo where we process your real invoices" is clear.
3. Remove the last objection in the CTA itself. "No credit card" kills payment fear. "15 minutes" kills time fear. "Cancel anytime" kills commitment fear.
4. Match the CTA to the buyer's stage. Do not ask for a demo in an awareness post. Do not ask for a newsletter signup in a bottom-funnel case study.
5. Never use fake urgency. If you say "5 spots left," there better be 5 spots left.
