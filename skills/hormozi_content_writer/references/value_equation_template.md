# Value Equation Template

## The Formula

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort/Sacrifice)**

To maximize value: increase the top, decrease the bottom.

---

## Fill-In-The-Blank Template

### 1. Dream Outcome
What is the specific, measurable result your buyer wants?

> [BUYER ROLE] wants to [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] so they can [BUSINESS IMPACT].
> Right now they get [CURRENT STATE]. After: [DESIRED STATE].

### 2. Perceived Likelihood
What proof exists that this outcome is real and achievable?

> - Customer result: [COMPANY NAME] achieved [SPECIFIC NUMBER] in [TIMEFRAME].
> - Data point: [X]% of customers hit [OUTCOME] within [TIME].
> - Case study: [COMPANY] went from [BEFORE] to [AFTER].
> - Social proof: [NUMBER] companies already use this.
> - Third-party validation: [AWARD/CERTIFICATION/PRESS MENTION].

### 3. Time Delay
How fast do they see results?

> - Time to first value: [X DAYS/HOURS].
> - Full implementation: [X WEEKS].
> - Compare to alternative: [COMPETITOR/OLD WAY] takes [X MONTHS]. We take [X DAYS].

### 4. Effort and Sacrifice
What do they NOT have to do?

> - No [PAINFUL TASK] required.
> - Works with [EXISTING TOOL THEY ALREADY USE].
> - Setup takes [X MINUTES/HOURS], not [X WEEKS/MONTHS].
> - They keep [THING THEY VALUE] while gaining [NEW CAPABILITY].

## Usage Notes

When applying this template:
1. Fill in every field with real specifics. Vague answers mean vague copy.
2. The Dream Outcome drives everything. Get this wrong and nothing else matters.
3. Stack at least 3 proof points. One is anecdote. Three is a pattern.
4. Time Delay is your secret weapon. If you are faster than alternatives, lead with speed.
5. Effort/Sacrifice removals should name the painful thing the buyer dreads. "No migration required" hits harder than "easy setup."
