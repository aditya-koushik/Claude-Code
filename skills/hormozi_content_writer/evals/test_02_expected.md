# Expected Output Characteristics: Test 02 (Edge Case)

## Must Exhibit

1. **Dream Outcome reframed from technical to business impact:** Must translate "40 hours/month to 3 hours/month" into a business outcome (e.g., "your engineering team stops babysitting integrations" or "reclaim 37 hours/month for actual product work"). NOT just restating the technical stat.
2. **CTO-appropriate tone:** Direct, technical credibility without marketing fluff. Must feel like a peer conversation, not a sales pitch. CTOs detect and reject obvious persuasion tactics.
3. **At least 2 specific numbers used:** From the brief stats. Must not round or soften them.
4. **Fortune 500 proof point used:** Must reference the bank case study with the 94% stat. Enterprise buyers trust enterprise references.
5. **Time delay addressed:** Must mention 6-week migration or similar timeframe so the CTO knows the commitment.
6. **SOC 2 mentioned naturally:** Security compliance referenced but not as the lead — it's table stakes for CTOs, not a differentiator.
7. **Single CTA:** "Schedule an architecture review" or equivalent. Architecture review framing is critical — CTOs don't "book demos", they "review architectures."
8. **Word count:** 150-300 words.

## Must NOT Exhibit

1. No banned words
2. No em-dashes
3. No "exciting" or emotionally charged language that would alienate a technical audience
4. No feature lists without business context (don't just list "200+ connectors" — tie it to time saved)
5. No generic enterprise buzzwords ("digital transformation", "next-generation platform")
6. No hedging
7. Content should not read identically to test_01 output — the tone should be noticeably more technical and peer-level
