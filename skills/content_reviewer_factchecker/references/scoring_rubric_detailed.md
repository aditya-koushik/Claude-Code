# Detailed Scoring Rubric

## Overview

Every piece of content is scored across 6 dimensions. Each dimension gets a raw score from 0-10. The composite score is the weighted average. Content must score 8.0 or above to pass.

**Weights:**
- Factual Accuracy: 30%
- Logical Coherence: 20%
- Platform Optimization: 15%
- Tone and Voice: 15%
- Grammar and Mechanics: 10%
- Asset Readiness: 10%

---

## Dimension 1: Factual Accuracy (Weight: 30%)

### Score 1-2: Poor
Every claim is unverified or wrong. Numbers are fabricated. Company names are misspelled or attributed incorrectly.

**Example of a 2:**
> "Google's new AI tool processes 10 billion documents per second, making it the fastest system ever built by a factor of 1000x."
>
> **Problems:** Unverifiable claim. No source. The number is implausible. "Factor of 1000x" is unsupported.

### Score 3-4: Below Average
Some claims are correct but key statistics lack sources. At least one factual error exists. Numbers feel round and estimated rather than precise.

**Example of a 4:**
> "Most enterprises waste about 40% of their IT budget on legacy systems. Companies like Microsoft and Salesforce have been pushing cloud migration for years."
>
> **Problems:** "About 40%" is unsourced and vague. "For years" is imprecise. The claim is directionally correct but unverified.

### Score 5-6: Average
Claims are generally accurate but 1-2 statistics cannot be verified. Attributions are correct. No obvious fabrications, but the content relies on commonly repeated claims without primary sources.

**Example of a 6:**
> "Gartner reports that 75% of enterprises will shift from piloting AI to operationalizing it by 2027. This aligns with the $200B+ being invested in AI infrastructure globally."
>
> **Problems:** Gartner citation is directionally correct but the exact percentage needs verification. "$200B+" is vague. Both claims are plausible but not pinned to specific reports.

### Score 7-8: Good
All major claims are verifiable. Statistics come from named sources. Company references are accurate. Minor claims may be common knowledge without citation, but nothing is fabricated.

**Example of an 8:**
> "According to McKinsey's 2025 Global AI Survey, 65% of organizations now regularly use generative AI, up from 33% in 2023. Salesforce reported $9.4B in Q3 2025 revenue, driven partly by their Einstein AI features."
>
> **Problems:** Minimal. Both citations are specific and verifiable. The causal link ("driven partly by") is slightly soft but hedged appropriately.

### Score 9-10: Excellent
Every claim is verified against a primary source. Statistics include the specific report, date, and context. No claim is unsupported. The content could withstand a journalist's fact-check.

**Example of a 10:**
> "Cloudwell reduced mean time to detection from 23 minutes to 47 seconds after deploying our monitoring tool in March 2026. Their engineering team confirmed this in a published case study on their blog dated April 2, 2026."
>
> **Why it scores 10:** Specific numbers. Named company. Named source. Date. Verifiable.

---

## Dimension 2: Logical Coherence (Weight: 20%)

### Score 1-2: Poor
No clear thesis. Points contradict each other. Conclusions do not follow from premises. The piece reads like random sentences assembled together.

**Example of a 2:**
> "AI will replace all jobs. Companies should hire more AI engineers. The future of work is human creativity."
>
> **Problems:** Three contradictory claims in sequence. No logical thread connecting them.

### Score 3-4: Below Average
A thesis exists but the argument has gaps. Some points support the thesis; others are tangential. The conclusion is a non-sequitur or restates the thesis without building on it.

**Example of a 4:**
> "Enterprise AI adoption is accelerating. Our product uses advanced NLP. Customer satisfaction scores are up across the industry. You should book a demo."
>
> **Problems:** The leap from industry trends to "book a demo" is unsupported. The NLP mention is disconnected.

### Score 5-6: Average
Clear thesis with supporting points, but the logical chain has 1-2 weak links. Some points could support the thesis more directly. The conclusion follows but feels expected rather than earned.

### Score 7-8: Good
Strong thesis. Every paragraph supports it. Logical flow from problem to evidence to conclusion. One minor logical leap at most.

### Score 9-10: Excellent
Airtight argument. Every sentence builds toward the conclusion. No logical fallacies. The reader arrives at the conclusion before the writer states it, because the reasoning is that clean.

---

## Dimension 3: Platform Optimization (Weight: 15%)

### Score 1-2: Poor
Content is a wall of text with no formatting. No hook. No hashtags. Wrong length for the platform. Would get zero engagement.

### Score 3-4: Below Average
Some formatting attempted. Hook exists but is weak. Hashtags present but wrong count. Length is off by 50%+.

**Example of a 4 (LinkedIn):**
> A 600-word post with no line breaks, 3 hashtags buried in the middle, and the hook starts with "I am excited to share..."
>
> **Problems:** Too long. Hashtags in wrong place and wrong count. Hook is a cliche opener.

### Score 5-6: Average
Correct basic structure. Hook exists. Hashtags at the end. But the hook is generic, the formatting has inconsistencies, and the length is slightly off.

### Score 7-8: Good
Strong hook. Correct formatting for the platform. 8-10 hashtags at the end (LinkedIn) or 5-8 in final tweet (Twitter). Correct length. Mobile-friendly line breaks. Minor optimization opportunities remain.

### Score 9-10: Excellent
Perfect platform format. Hook would stop the scroll. Line breaks create ideal mobile reading experience. Hashtag mix of broad and niche. Length is in the sweet spot. Every element is optimized.

---

## Dimension 4: Tone and Voice (Weight: 15%)

### Score 1-2: Poor
Reads like a press release or academic paper. Passive voice throughout. Uses banned words freely. No personality. Could have been written by any generic AI.

**Example of a 2:**
> "In today's rapidly evolving landscape, it is important to note that organizations must leverage comprehensive AI solutions to unlock their full potential and drive dynamic growth."
>
> **Problems:** Every banned word in one sentence. Passive voice. Zero personality. Pure AI slop.

### Score 3-4: Below Average
Some personality, but inconsistent. Shifts between formal and casual. Uses 2-3 banned words. Occasional passive voice. Reads like a human edited an AI draft poorly.

### Score 5-6: Average
Consistent tone throughout. Mostly active voice. One banned word or hedging phrase slipped through. Personality exists but is generic. Could be any business writer.

### Score 7-8: Good
Distinct voice. Active, direct, opinionated. No banned words. Short sentences. Sounds like a specific person, not a template. One small tonal inconsistency.

### Score 9-10: Excellent
Unmistakably Bit Foundry AI voice. Direct, confrontational, zero hedging. Short declarative sentences. Fragments for punch. Names real companies. Uses real numbers. First person sparingly but powerfully. No reader would guess AI wrote this.

**Example of a 10:**
> "Your AI vendor wants you to buy a platform. You need a workflow. Platforms sit on shelves. Workflows run inside your business every day. Salesforce figured this out in 2024. Most of their competitors still have not."
>
> **Why it scores 10:** Direct. Short sentences. Fragment for punch ("Workflows run inside your business every day."). Names a real company. Takes a position. Zero hedging.

---

## Dimension 5: Grammar and Mechanics (Weight: 10%)

### Score 1-2: Poor
Multiple spelling errors. Broken sentences. Inconsistent tense. Formatting artifacts visible.

### Score 3-4: Below Average
1-2 spelling or grammar errors. Some punctuation issues. A dangling modifier.

### Score 5-6: Average
Grammatically correct but reads stiffly. Technically right but not polished.

### Score 7-8: Good
Clean grammar. Consistent tense. Proper punctuation. All proper nouns spelled correctly. Minor style inconsistency at most.

### Score 9-10: Excellent
Flawless mechanics. Every sentence is tight. No extra words. Punctuation is deliberate. Technical terms used correctly. Zero errors of any kind.

---

## Dimension 6: Asset Readiness (Weight: 10%)

### Score 1-2: Poor
No visual asset exists. Or the asset is a generic stock photo with no connection to the content.

**Automatic REVISE trigger:** Missing asset = automatic REVISE regardless of other scores.

### Score 3-4: Below Average
Asset exists but is wrong size for the platform, has illegible text, or does not match the content topic.

### Score 5-6: Average
Asset matches the topic and is correct size. But it is generic. No brand elements. Does not add information beyond what the text says.

### Score 7-8: Good
Asset is on-brand, correct size, and adds value. It reinforces the key message or visualizes data from the post. Minor design improvements possible.

### Score 9-10: Excellent
Asset is compelling on its own. Brand-consistent. Adds information the text does not contain (chart, framework diagram, comparison). Would work as a standalone image. Proper dimensions for the target platform.

---

## Composite Score Calculation

```
Composite = (Factual x 0.30) + (Logical x 0.20) + (Platform x 0.15) + (Tone x 0.15) + (Grammar x 0.10) + (Asset x 0.10)
```

**Example:**
- Factual: 9 x 0.30 = 2.70
- Logical: 8 x 0.20 = 1.60
- Platform: 8 x 0.15 = 1.20
- Tone: 9 x 0.15 = 1.35
- Grammar: 9 x 0.10 = 0.90
- Asset: 7 x 0.10 = 0.70
- **Composite: 8.45 = APPROVE**

---

## Decision Thresholds

| Composite Score | Decision | Action |
|----------------|----------|--------|
| 8.0 - 10.0 | APPROVE | Send to publish queue |
| 6.0 - 7.9 | REVISE | Send back with specific notes |
| Below 6.0 | REJECT | Do not revise. Start over. |

**Override rules:**
- Any unverified factual claim = REVISE regardless of composite score
- Missing visual asset = REVISE regardless of composite score
- Any banned word present = REVISE regardless of composite score
- 3 revision cycles without reaching 8.0 = Escalate to human review
