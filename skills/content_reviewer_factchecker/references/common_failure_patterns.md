# Common Failure Patterns

## 10 Most Common Quality Gate Failures

These are the patterns that cause content to score below 8.0 most often. Each entry includes what the failure looks like, why it happens, and how to fix it.

---

### Failure 1: Unverified Statistics

**What it looks like:**
> "Studies show that 90% of enterprises will adopt AI by 2027."

**Why it fails:** "Studies show" is not a source. Which study? By whom? Published when? Vague attribution is the single most common reason content gets flagged. It tanks the Factual Accuracy score.

**How to spot it:**
- "Studies show..." / "Research indicates..." / "According to experts..."
- Round numbers without a source (90%, 80%, 50%)
- Statistics that sound too perfect to be true
- "A recent report found..." without naming the report

**How to fix it:**
- Name the source: "McKinsey's 2025 Global AI Survey found that 65% of organizations regularly use generative AI."
- If you cannot find the source, cut the statistic. A post without stats is better than a post with made-up stats.
- Replace vague stats with specific customer results you can verify.

---

### Failure 2: Passive Voice Creep

**What it looks like:**
> "The platform was designed to be used by enterprise teams. Results were achieved within 30 days. The system is being adopted by leading companies."

**Why it fails:** Three passive constructions in a row. The writing sounds corporate, distant, and lifeless. It tanks Tone/Voice and Grammar scores.

**How to spot it:**
- "was [verb]ed" / "is being [verb]ed" / "were [verb]ed"
- Sentences where the subject receives the action instead of performing it
- Any sentence where you cannot immediately identify who is doing the action

**How to fix it:**
- "We built the platform for enterprise teams. They hit results in 30 days. Leading companies are adopting it now."
- Every sentence needs a clear subject doing something.

---

### Failure 3: Missing Hashtags

**What it looks like:**
A LinkedIn post that ends with the CTA and nothing else. Or a post with 3 hashtags when the requirement is 8-10.

**Why it fails:** LinkedIn posts require 8-10 hashtags on the final line. Twitter closing tweets need 5-8. This is a hard rule. Missing or insufficient hashtags = automatic Platform Optimization penalty.

**How to spot it:**
- Post ends without a hashtag line
- Fewer than 8 hashtags for LinkedIn, fewer than 5 for Twitter
- Hashtags scattered in the body instead of at the end
- Commas between hashtags

**How to fix it:**
- Add exactly 8-10 hashtags for LinkedIn, 5-8 for Twitter
- Place them on the very last line
- Space-separated, no commas
- Mix broad (#AI #SaaS) with niche (#EnterpriseAI #BuildInPublic)

---

### Failure 4: Wrong Tone (Too Soft/Corporate)

**What it looks like:**
> "We are pleased to announce our innovative solution that helps organizations navigate the complex challenges of digital transformation in today's rapidly evolving business environment."

**Why it fails:** This is corporate press release tone. The Bit Foundry voice is direct, confrontational, and opinionated. This sentence hedges, generalizes, and says nothing specific. It tanks Tone/Voice instantly.

**How to spot it:**
- "We are pleased/excited/thrilled to..."
- "Innovative solution" / "cutting-edge technology"
- "Navigate the complex challenges of..."
- "In today's rapidly evolving..."
- Any sentence that could appear in any company's press release

**How to fix it:**
- "Most digital transformation projects fail. Here is why yours will too, unless you change one thing."
- Take a position. Name a specific problem. Be direct about what is wrong.

---

### Failure 5: Too Long or Too Short

**What it looks like:**
- LinkedIn post at 500+ words (too long) or 60 words (too short)
- Twitter thread with tweets exceeding 280 characters
- A post that buries the point in paragraph 4

**Why it fails:** LinkedIn sweet spot is 150-300 words. Too long and readers drop off. Too short and the algorithm reads it as low-effort. Either way, Platform Optimization takes a hit.

**How to spot it:**
- Word count outside the 150-300 range for LinkedIn
- Any tweet over 280 characters
- Multiple paragraphs that repeat the same point

**How to fix it:**
- If too long: cut the weakest paragraph. Tighten sentences. Remove redundancy.
- If too short: develop the insight section. Add one proof point or example.
- Count words. This is not subjective.

---

### Failure 6: Missing CTA

**What it looks like:**
A post that delivers good content but ends abruptly with no call to action. The reader finishes and has nothing to do next.

**Why it fails:** Every post needs a CTA. For LinkedIn, this is usually a question that drives comments. For conversion posts, it is a specific next step. Missing CTA = the content did work but captured zero value from it.

**How to spot it:**
- Post ends with the final insight and nothing else
- CTA is vague: "Let me know what you think"
- CTA asks for too many things: "Like, comment, share, and follow"

**How to fix it:**
- End with one specific question: "What is the most expensive manual process in your business?"
- Or one specific action: "Book a 15-minute demo."
- One CTA. Not two. Not three.

---

### Failure 7: AI-Sounding Language

**What it looks like:**
> "In the realm of enterprise AI, it is important to note that organizations must delve into comprehensive strategies to harness the full potential of these dynamic tools."

**Why it fails:** This sentence contains 5 banned words (realm, delve, comprehensive, harness, dynamic) and one AI tell-phrase ("it is important to note"). Any reader who has used ChatGPT will immediately recognize this as AI-generated. Destroys credibility.

**How to spot it:**
- Banned words: delve, realm, landscape, cutting-edge, game-changer, paradigm, synergy, leverage, utilize, harness, comprehensive, dynamic, revolutionize, unlock
- Tell-phrases: "it is important to note," "in today's rapidly evolving," "it is worth noting that"
- Em-dashes used for parenthetical asides
- Perfectly balanced paragraph lengths
- Lists of exactly 5 items with parallel structure

**How to fix it:**
- Run a banned word scan. Zero tolerance.
- Replace with simple words: delve = dig into, harness = use, comprehensive = complete
- Rewrite any sentence that "sounds like ChatGPT"

---

### Failure 8: Hedging Words

**What it looks like:**
> "This might potentially help some organizations improve their operations somewhat."

**Why it fails:** "Might," "potentially," "some," "somewhat" all hedge. The sentence commits to nothing. Bit Foundry voice is direct and certain. If you are not sure enough to state something directly, cut it.

**How to spot it:**
- might, perhaps, possibly, potentially, arguably
- "some" when you mean "most" or "all"
- "could help" instead of "helps" or "will help"
- "it seems like" instead of "it is"
- "in many cases" instead of stating the specific case

**How to fix it:**
- Cut the hedge word and state directly: "This helps organizations improve operations."
- If you genuinely are not sure, cut the entire claim. Do not hedge. Either commit or delete.

---

### Failure 9: Missing Visual Asset

**What it looks like:**
A post submitted without any accompanying image, chart, diagram, or carousel.

**Why it fails:** Every post must have a visual asset. This is a hard rule. Missing asset triggers an automatic REVISE decision regardless of all other scores.

**How to spot it:**
- No image file attached to the submission
- A generic stock photo that has no connection to the content
- An image with text too small to read on mobile

**How to fix it:**
- Create a visual that reinforces the key message
- Options: quote card, data chart, framework diagram, comparison graphic, carousel
- Verify the image is correct dimensions for the target platform
- Text on images must be readable at mobile size

---

### Failure 10: Broken Formatting

**What it looks like:**
- LinkedIn post with no line breaks between paragraphs
- Bold markers (**text**) appearing as raw markdown instead of rendering
- Hashtags with commas between them
- Thread tweets not numbered
- Mixed formatting styles within one post

**Why it fails:** Broken formatting makes content hard to read and signals lack of quality control. It drops the Platform Optimization score and makes even good content perform poorly.

**How to spot it:**
- Wall of text with no white space
- Raw markdown characters visible
- Inconsistent spacing between paragraphs
- Hashtags formatted with commas: "#AI, #SaaS, #Leadership"
- Missing blank lines between the hook and body

**How to fix it:**
- Add a blank line after every 1-3 sentences
- Verify bold rendering on the target platform
- Remove commas between hashtags
- Number all tweets in a thread
- Preview the post as a reader would see it before submitting

---

## Quick Failure Detection Checklist

Run these 10 checks in order. If any check fails, stop and fix before proceeding.

| # | Check | Pass Criteria |
|---|-------|--------------|
| 1 | Banned word scan | Zero banned words |
| 2 | Statistic sources | Every stat has a named source |
| 3 | Passive voice scan | Under 5% passive voice |
| 4 | Word count | 150-300 words (LinkedIn) |
| 5 | Hashtag count | 8-10 (LinkedIn), 5-8 (Twitter final tweet) |
| 6 | CTA present | One clear CTA at the end |
| 7 | Asset attached | Visual asset exists and matches content |
| 8 | Hedge word scan | Zero hedge words |
| 9 | Formatting check | Proper line breaks, no raw markdown |
| 10 | Hook strength | First line is a standalone attention-grabber |
