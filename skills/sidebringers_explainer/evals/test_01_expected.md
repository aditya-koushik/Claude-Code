# Expected Output Characteristics: Test 01

## Must Exhibit

1. **At least 1 concrete analogy:** E.g., "RAG is like giving your AI a research assistant" or "It's like an open-book exam instead of a memory test." The analogy must accurately map to how RAG works.
2. **Zero unexplained jargon:** Terms like "vector representation", "embeddings", "inference time", "parametric knowledge" must be either eliminated or explained in plain language.
3. **Business impact stated:** Must answer "why should a CEO care?" — e.g., "This means your AI gives answers based on YOUR company's data, not just what it learned during training. Fewer hallucinations. More accurate answers. Less risk."
4. **Core mechanism preserved:** The explanation must convey that RAG = AI looks up real information before answering (not just guessing from memory). This is the essential truth that cannot be lost.
5. **150-300 words:** Concise. Not a lecture.
6. **Grade 5-7 reading level:** Short sentences. Simple words. No compound-complex sentences.
7. **Sidebrinders framework visible:** Concept identified, analogy used, business impact framed, language simplified.

## Must NOT Exhibit

1. Technical jargon left unexplained ("vector database", "embeddings", "top-k retrieval")
2. Analogy that misrepresents how RAG works (e.g., "RAG trains the AI on your data" — wrong, RAG retrieves, it doesn't retrain)
3. Over 500 words
4. Missing business impact ("so what?" left unanswered)
5. Patronizing tone ("to put it simply for you...")
6. Hedging language ("might", "could potentially")
