# Test Input 02: Edge Case — Explain Vector Embeddings (No Obvious Analogy)

## Task
Simplify vector embeddings for non-technical business leaders. This is deliberately hard because there is no obvious everyday analogy for representing meaning as numbers in high-dimensional space.

## Technical Concept
**Vector Embeddings**

Technical explanation: Vector embeddings are dense, fixed-length numerical representations of data (text, images, audio) in a high-dimensional space, typically 768 to 4096 dimensions. They are generated by neural networks trained on large datasets. The key property is that semantically similar items are mapped to nearby points in the embedding space — measured by cosine similarity or Euclidean distance. This enables semantic search: finding content by meaning rather than keyword matching. Embeddings are fundamental to RAG, recommendation systems, anomaly detection, and clustering. The embedding model converts unstructured data into a format that machines can perform mathematical operations on.

## Target Audience
A non-technical enterprise CEO making a $500K AI platform purchase decision. They need to understand what embeddings ARE and WHY they matter for their purchase, not how to implement them.

## Desired Output
A 150-300 word explanation with business framing. Must work for someone who has never written code.
