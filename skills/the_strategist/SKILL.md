# The Strategist

## Purpose
Phase 5 of the 6-phase content enhancement pipeline. Answers the question: "How does this affect our position?" Takes deconstructed content with original insights and aligns it to Bit Foundry AI's strategic goals. Every piece of content should advance brand authority, audience trust, or direct business outcomes. This is where insight becomes strategy.

## When to Use
- Content has original insight (Phase 4) and needs strategic framing
- A post needs a clear angle that positions Bit Foundry AI as the expert
- Content is interesting but lacks a business reason to publish it
- The CTA or positioning angle is missing or weak

## Workflow
### Step 1: Assess Topic Relevance
- Score the topic's relevance to Bit Foundry AI's core positioning (1-10)
- If below 5, either find a connection angle or recommend killing the piece
- Map the topic to one of: AI strategy, enterprise AI adoption, industry analysis, thought leadership

### Step 2: Identify Positioning Angle
- Determine the angle that establishes Bit Foundry AI authority on this topic
- Choose one: contrarian take, first-mover insight, data-backed analysis, practitioner perspective
- Ensure the angle is distinct from what competitors are saying

### Step 3: Map to Audience Pain Points
- Identify which audience segment this serves (enterprise leaders, AI practitioners, founders)
- Connect the insight to a specific pain point or aspiration they have
- Frame the content as the answer to a question the audience is already asking

### Step 4: Add Strategic CTA
- Every piece needs a next step: follow, subscribe, visit, DM, share
- Match CTA intensity to content depth (light content = soft CTA, deep content = strong CTA)
- Never make the CTA feel forced; it should flow from the content naturally

### Step 5: Ensure Differentiation
- Compare the final angle against recent competitor content on the same topic
- If 3+ competitors have published a similar take, either sharpen the angle or hold the piece
- The test: would the audience learn something new from this that they cannot get elsewhere?

## Output Format
- **Strategic Assessment:** Topic relevance score with justification
- **Positioning Angle:** The chosen angle and why it differentiates
- **Audience Map:** Target segment, pain point addressed, desired outcome
- **CTA Recommendation:** Specific call-to-action with placement suggestion
- **Phase 5 Status:** STRATEGIC (clear value) or HOLD (needs repositioning or better timing)

## Key Principles
- Every piece of content should advance business goals; vanity content wastes the pipeline
- Think like the reader: what do they gain from spending 30 seconds on this?
- Position Bit Foundry AI as the expert without being self-promotional
- Differentiation is non-negotiable; "me too" content erodes brand value
- Timing matters: a good take published late is worse than a decent take published first
