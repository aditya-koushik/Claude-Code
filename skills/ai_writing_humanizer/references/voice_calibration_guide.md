# Voice Calibration Guide

## The Bit Foundry AI Voice

This guide defines the exact voice characteristics that make content sound like it came from Bit Foundry AI. Use this as the reference standard when humanizing any piece of content.

---

## Core Voice Characteristics

### 1. Direct and Confrontational
State positions without softening. Do not ask for permission to have an opinion. Say what is true, even when it is uncomfortable.

### 2. Short Declarative Sentences
Average sentence length: 12-15 words. Maximum: 20 words. One idea per sentence. Period. Next idea.

### 3. Fragments for Punch
Use sentence fragments strategically. "Not a chance." "Same team. Same product. Different result." "Zero." Fragments create rhythm and emphasis.

### 4. Enterprise/Business Lens
Every point connects to business outcomes: revenue, cost, time, competitive advantage. Never abstract. Always concrete. The reader is a CEO, VP, or founder. They think in dollars and quarters.

### 5. Names Real Companies
Reference specific companies by name. "Salesforce" not "a leading CRM company." "Deloitte" not "a Big 4 firm." Real names signal real knowledge.

### 6. Uses Real Numbers
"347 customers" not "hundreds of customers." "82% reduction" not "significant reduction." "$340,000 saved" not "substantial savings." Specific numbers prove you have done the work.

### 7. First Person Sparingly but Powerfully
Use "I" for personal experience and conviction. Use "you" to address the reader directly. Avoid "we" when possible. Never use "one" as a pronoun.

---

## The Banned List

These words and constructions never appear in Bit Foundry content:

**Banned words:** delve, dynamic, unlock, revolutionize, harness, comprehensive, landscape, paradigm, synergy, leverage (as verb), utilize, realm, cutting-edge, game-changer, innovative (without proof), transformative (without proof)

**Banned constructions:** em-dashes, "it's important to note," "in today's rapidly evolving," "it's worth noting that," "moreover," "furthermore," "in conclusion," "not only...but also"

**Banned hedges:** might, perhaps, possibly, potentially, arguably, somewhat, to a certain extent

## 5 Before/After Examples

### Example 1: Product Description

**BEFORE (AI voice):**
> In today's rapidly evolving enterprise landscape, organizations are increasingly looking to leverage comprehensive AI solutions to unlock operational efficiencies and drive transformative outcomes. Our platform -- designed with cutting-edge technology -- helps businesses harness the power of artificial intelligence to revolutionize their workflows.

**AFTER (Bit Foundry voice):**
> Most enterprises waste 200+ hours per month on manual data processing. Our platform cuts that by 82%. Meridian Logistics went from 14-day invoice cycles to 3-day cycles in the first month. No custom code. No 6-month implementation. Live in a week.

**What changed:**
- Killed 7 banned words (landscape, leverage, comprehensive, unlock, transformative, cutting-edge, harness, revolutionize)
- Removed em-dash
- Replaced vague claims with specific numbers (200+ hours, 82%, 14 days to 3 days)
- Named a real company (Meridian Logistics)
- Cut word count from 49 to 42 while adding 4x more information

---

### Example 2: Industry Trend Post

**BEFORE (AI voice):**
> It's important to note that the AI industry is undergoing a paradigm shift. Furthermore, it's worth noting that enterprises that fail to adapt may find themselves at a significant competitive disadvantage. Many experts believe that AI adoption will accelerate considerably in the coming years.

**AFTER (Bit Foundry voice):**
> 73% of enterprises will run AI agents in production by 2027. That is not a prediction from a blog post. That is McKinsey's latest survey of 1,400 companies. The ones who wait will spend 3x more to catch up. Every quarter of delay costs market share that does not come back.

**What changed:**
- Cut "important to note," "worth noting," "paradigm," "furthermore"
- Replaced "many experts believe" with a specific source (McKinsey)
- Added real numbers (73%, 1,400 companies, 3x, every quarter)
- Changed passive hedging to direct assertion
- Word count stayed similar but information density tripled

---

### Example 3: Case Study Introduction

**BEFORE (AI voice):**
> We are pleased to share an exciting case study that demonstrates how our innovative platform helped a leading real estate company achieve remarkable results through our comprehensive suite of AI-powered tools. The organization was able to significantly improve their operational efficiency and drive substantial revenue growth.

**AFTER (Bit Foundry voice):**
> Desert Sun Realty went from $2.1M to $6.8M in annual revenue. Same 30 agents. Same Dubai market. The only thing that changed was how they qualified leads. Here is exactly what we did.

**What changed:**
- Cut "pleased to share," "exciting," "innovative," "comprehensive," "remarkable," "significantly," "substantially"
- Named the company (Desert Sun Realty) and location (Dubai)
- Replaced all vague qualifiers with exact numbers ($2.1M to $6.8M, 30 agents)
- Changed "organization was able to" (passive) to direct active voice
- Cut 48 words to 34 while telling a more complete story

### Example 4: LinkedIn Post Opening

**BEFORE (AI voice):**
> I've been reflecting on the dynamic shifts happening across the enterprise software landscape, and I think it's arguably one of the most transformative periods we've ever seen. As someone who has been working in this space for several years, I'd like to share some thoughts on where things might be heading.

**AFTER (Bit Foundry voice):**
> The system of record is dead.

For 20 years, Salesforce, SAP, and Oracle sold enterprises a simple idea: put all your data in one place. It worked. Then AI agents showed up and broke the model.

Here is what replaces it.

**What changed:**
- Cut "reflecting," "dynamic," "landscape," "arguably," "transformative," "might"
- Replaced 52 words of throat-clearing with a 6-word hook
- Named specific companies (Salesforce, SAP, Oracle) instead of vague industry references
- Changed "I think...might be heading" to "Here is what replaces it." Certainty, not hedging.
- The before says nothing in 52 words. The after makes a bold claim and sets up a framework in 44 words.

### Example 5: CTA Section

**BEFORE (AI voice):**
> If you're interested in learning more about how our platform could potentially help your organization navigate the complex challenges of digital transformation, we'd love to schedule a brief conversation to explore how we might be able to assist you on your journey.

**AFTER (Bit Foundry voice):**
> Book a 15-minute demo. We will process 50 of your real invoices live. If we cannot beat your current process, the call was free anyway.

**What changed:**
- Cut "interested in learning more," "potentially," "navigate the complex challenges," "digital transformation," "explore how we might"
- Replaced vague invitation with specific action (15-minute demo)
- Added a concrete deliverable (process 50 real invoices)
- Added a risk reversal (the call was free anyway)
- 44 words became 27 words. The 27 words will convert 10x better.

## Voice Calibration Checklist

Before any content passes this skill, verify all of the following:

| Check | Requirement |
|-------|------------|
| Sentence length | Average under 15 words. Max 20. |
| Fragments | At least 2 per piece for rhythm |
| Banned words | Zero. No exceptions. |
| Em-dashes | Zero. No exceptions. |
| Hedge words | Zero. No exceptions. |
| Specific numbers | At least 3 per piece |
| Named companies | At least 1 per piece |
| Active voice | 95%+ of sentences |
| "You" and "I" | Used instead of "we" or "one" |
| First sentence | Would stop a CEO from scrolling |
| Last sentence | Most memorable line in the piece |
| Reading level | Grade 5 or below |

If any check fails, rewrite the failing section before proceeding.
