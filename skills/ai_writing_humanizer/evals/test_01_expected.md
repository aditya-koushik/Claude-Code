# Expected Output Characteristics: Test 01

## Must Exhibit

1. **All AI patterns detected and listed:** Must flag: "In today's rapidly evolving", "it's important to note", "truly far-reaching and multifaceted", "Let's delve into", "revolutionizing", "unlocking unprecedented insights", "It could be argued", "paradigm", "harness", "comprehensive approach", "Perhaps the most compelling", "leverage", "In conclusion". At least 12 AI patterns flagged.
2. **Banned words removed:** Must catch: "landscape", "delve", "revolutionizing", "unlocking", "paradigm", "harness", "comprehensive", "Perhaps", "leverage". That is 9 banned words.
3. **Em-dash removed:** "automation — combined with AI — is" must be rewritten without em-dashes.
4. **Filler transitions removed:** "In today's rapidly evolving", "It's important to note", "In conclusion", "However, it's equally important to acknowledge" all gone.
5. **Hedging eliminated:** "It could be argued", "Perhaps the most compelling", "it's equally important to acknowledge" replaced with direct statements.
6. **List-heavy structure broken:** The bullet points and numbered lists should be converted to flowing prose or reduced. AI loves lists; humans write paragraphs.
7. **At least 2 personality markers added:** Rhetorical questions, specific numbers, first-person statements, or contrarian takes inserted.
8. **Reading level Grade 5 or below.**
9. **Changelog included** with counts of patterns removed and markers added.

## Must NOT Exhibit

1. Any of the 9 banned words remaining
2. Any em-dashes remaining
3. "In conclusion" or any summary transition
4. Hedging language ("it could be", "perhaps", "arguably")
5. The exact same list structure (bullets + numbered list)
6. Word-for-word preservation of AI-patterned sentences
7. Output still reads as "AI-generated" to a human reader
