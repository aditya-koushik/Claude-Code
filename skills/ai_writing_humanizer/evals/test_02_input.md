## Content

Nobody talks about the real cost of SaaS sprawl.

It is worth noting that the average enterprise now runs over 300 applications. Each one stores a slice of your data. Each one has its own API. Each one sends you a separate invoice.

I have seen CTOs literally print out their SaaS invoice stack. It fills a table.

The integration tax is what kills you. Moreover, every connection between two tools requires maintenance. Furthermore, when one vendor updates their API, your integration breaks. Additionally, you need an engineer to fix it within hours or your data pipeline goes stale.

Here is the math that changed my mind. A mid-market company with 200 tools spends roughly $150,000 per year just keeping integrations alive. That is one full-time engineer doing nothing but API babysitting.

It could be said that the solution is consolidation, but it's arguably more nuanced than that. The key is not fewer tools. The key is an orchestration layer that makes the number of tools irrelevant.

The companies I talk to that have figured this out share one trait. They stopped thinking about their tech stack as a collection of tools and started thinking about it as a data flow.

What does your data flow look like?
