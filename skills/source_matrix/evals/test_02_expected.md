# Expected Output Characteristics: Test 02 (Edge Case)

## Must Exhibit

1. **Gartner scored differently for predictions vs facts:** Must note that Gartner is Tier A for historical data and market sizing, but Tier B or C for predictions. The specific claim is a prediction. Blockchain prediction miss should be cited. Score should reflect this distinction (50-70 range for this specific claim type).
2. **IBM Research scored with vendor bias noted (55-75):** Good data backing (published paper, methodology), strong authority (IBM Research is legitimate), BUT massive conflict of interest (IBM rating their own product). Must flag this. Recommendation should be "Use only with independent corroboration."
3. **CB Insights scored with severe recency penalty (40-60):** Authority and track record are good. Data backing was strong at publication time. But recency score should be very low (5-10 out of 25) because the data is 3+ years old and the claim is about "current" trends. Must explicitly state that 2022 data cannot support 2025 claims.
4. **Context-dependent scoring demonstrated:** The evaluator must show it understands that credibility is claim-specific, not source-generic. Same source can score differently for different claim types.
5. **Prediction vs fact distinction made for Gartner:** Must articulate why predictions are inherently less reliable than historical data, even from reputable sources.
6. **Self-reporting bias flagged for IBM:** A company benchmarking its own product has inherent incentive to select favorable benchmarks or conditions.
7. **Math correct across all 3 sources.**
8. **Complete output format for all 3.**

## Must NOT Exhibit

1. Gartner scored 90+ just because it's Gartner (the claim type matters)
2. IBM scored 80+ despite being a self-report (conflict of interest must lower score)
3. CB Insights scored 80+ despite 3-year-old data being used for current claims
4. Treating all sources as equally credible across all claim types
5. Ignoring the prediction vs fact distinction
6. Math errors
