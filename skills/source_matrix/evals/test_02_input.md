# Test Input 02: Edge Case — Mixed Credibility Sources

## Task
Evaluate the following 3 sources. These are deliberately tricky — one is credible in one domain but not another, one is outdated but was once excellent, one is a company blog with surprisingly good data.

## Source 1
**Name:** Gartner
**Type:** Institution (analyst firm)
**URL:** gartner.com
**Claim being verified:** "Kubernetes adoption will reach 85% of enterprises by 2027"
**Context:** Published in their 2025 infrastructure forecast. However, the claim being evaluated is a PREDICTION, not a historical fact. Gartner's prediction track record is mixed (their 2020 predictions about blockchain adoption were significantly off).

## Source 2
**Name:** IBM Research Blog
**Type:** Company blog (vendor)
**URL:** research.ibm.com/blog
**Claim being verified:** "IBM's Granite model achieves 94% accuracy on enterprise document processing benchmarks"
**Context:** Blog post from IBM's own research team. They link to a published paper with methodology and benchmark details. However, IBM is claiming results about their own product.

## Source 3
**Name:** CB Insights "State of AI" Report (2022 edition)
**Type:** Institution (market research)
**URL:** cbinsights.com
**Claim being verified:** "AI funding reached $119 billion globally in 2022"
**Context:** This was excellent data when published in 2022. However, it is now 3+ years old. The AI funding landscape has changed dramatically since then. Being used to support a claim about "current AI funding trends."
