# Twitter/X Thread Structure Template

## Thread Anatomy

A thread is a sequence of connected tweets. Each tweet must stand alone as a complete thought AND connect to the next tweet in the sequence. The hook tweet does 80% of the work. If the hook fails, nobody reads tweet 2.

---

## Structure

```
1/ [HOOK TWEET] - Standalone. Max 280 chars. Must work as a solo tweet.

2/ [SETUP] - Context, background, or the problem.

3/ [POINT 1] - First key insight, data point, or story beat.

4/ [POINT 2] - Second key insight. Build on tweet 3.

5/ [POINT 3] - Third key insight. Peak of the thread.

6/ [CTA + HASHTAGS] - Call to action. Retweet/follow/reply. 5-8 hashtags.
```

**Rules:**
- Minimum 4 tweets. Maximum 12 for regular threads. (25 is the absolute cap, but shorter threads perform better.)
- Hook tweet must work on its own. If someone only sees tweet 1, they should still get value or be compelled to click.
- Each tweet is one complete thought. Never split a sentence across two tweets.
- No hashtags until the final tweet. 5-8 hashtags in the closing tweet.
- Number every tweet: "1/", "2/", etc.

## Annotated Example Thread 1: Teaching Framework

```
1/ 80% of AI implementations fail in the first year.

Not because the tech is bad. Because companies skip 3 steps that actually matter.

A thread:

2/ Step 1: Pick one workflow. Not "AI strategy." One specific process.

The company that automates invoice processing first will beat the company that builds an "AI roadmap" for 6 months.

Start small. Start specific.

3/ Step 2: Measure before you build.

How long does it take today? How much does it cost? How many errors?

If you do not have baseline numbers, you cannot prove ROI. And if you cannot prove ROI, the project gets killed in Q2.

4/ Step 3: Deploy to 10 users first. Not 10,000.

Run a 2-week pilot with your most skeptical team. If they adopt it, everyone will. If they reject it, you just saved 6 months of wasted rollout.

5/ The pattern across 40+ enterprise AI projects I have seen:

Tool-first companies: 20% success rate.
Workflow-first companies: 78% success rate.

The tool is the last decision. Not the first.

6/ If you are planning an AI project in 2026, save this thread.

Follow for more breakdowns on AI that works in real enterprises.

RT tweet 1 if this helped someone on your team.

#AI #EnterpriseAI #AIStrategy #SaaS #BuildInPublic #AIImplementation
```

**Annotation:**
- **Tweet 1:** Hook with a surprising stat + promise of a framework. Works standalone. Under 280 chars. "A thread:" signals more is coming.
- **Tweet 2-4:** One step per tweet. Each is a complete, actionable thought. No sentence crosses tweet boundaries.
- **Tweet 5:** Data summary that proves the framework. The thread's peak moment.
- **Tweet 6:** CTA (follow + RT) with 6 hashtags. Three specific asks: save, follow, RT.

## Annotated Example Thread 2: Story-Based

```
1/ Last month, our biggest enterprise client told us they were leaving.

What happened next saved the account and changed how we do business.

2/ The call came on a Tuesday at 4 PM.

"We like your product. But your competitor offered 40% lower pricing and faster onboarding. We are switching in 30 days."

3/ My first instinct: match the price. Discount. Do whatever it takes.

Instead, I asked one question: "What outcome do you need in the next 90 days that you are not getting today?"

4/ Silence. Then: "We need our team processing 500 invoices per day instead of 200. That is the real problem."

Their complaint was about price. Their problem was about throughput.

5/ We built a custom workflow in 72 hours. Hit 500 invoices per day by Friday.

The competitor could not do that. They had a cheaper tool. We had a better result.

6/ The client stayed. Signed a 2-year extension.

When a customer says "price," they mean "value." Give them the outcome and the price objection disappears.

7/ If you sell to enterprises, remember this:

They do not buy tools. They buy outcomes. Match the outcome. The price conversation ends.

Follow for more real stories from building an AI company.

#AI #SaaS #Sales #B2BSaaS #EnterpriseAI #Entrepreneurship #BuildInPublic
```

**Annotation:**
- **Tweet 1:** Story hook with tension (client leaving). Creates an open loop. Under 280 chars.
- **Tweet 2-3:** Story beats. Each tweet advances the narrative one step. Specific details (Tuesday, 4 PM, 40%) make it real.
- **Tweet 4:** The turning point. The real problem surfaces. This is where the reader has the "aha" moment.
- **Tweet 5:** The resolution. Specific numbers (500 invoices, 72 hours, Friday).
- **Tweet 6:** The outcome + lesson. Memorable one-liner format.
- **Tweet 7:** CTA with the universal takeaway. Follow request. 7 hashtags.

## Thread Type Templates

### The Listicle Thread
```
1/ [NUMBER] things I learned [DOING X].

Thread:

2/ 1. [LESSON]

[1-2 sentence explanation]

3/ 2. [LESSON]

[1-2 sentence explanation]

...

n-1/ [NUMBER]. [LESSON]

[1-2 sentence explanation]

n/ Save this thread. RT tweet 1 if any of these hit.

Follow for more [TOPIC].

[5-8 HASHTAGS]
```

### The Breakdown Thread
```
1/ [COMPANY/PRODUCT] just [DID SOMETHING].

Here is what it means for [AUDIENCE]. A breakdown:

2/ What happened: [FACTUAL SUMMARY in 1-2 sentences]

3/ Why it matters: [BUSINESS IMPACT in 2-3 sentences]

4/ What to do about it: [SPECIFIC ACTIONABLE ADVICE]

5/ The bigger picture: [TREND or PREDICTION]

6/ Follow for breakdowns that cut through the noise.

[5-8 HASHTAGS]
```

### The Contrarian Thread
```
1/ Unpopular opinion: [BOLD CLAIM].

Here is why. Thread:

2/ The common belief: [WHAT EVERYONE THINKS]

3/ The problem with that: [WHY IT IS WRONG + EVIDENCE]

4/ What actually works: [YOUR ALTERNATIVE + PROOF]

5/ The result: [SPECIFIC OUTCOME FROM DOING IT YOUR WAY]

6/ Agree or disagree? Reply with your take.

Follow for takes that challenge the status quo.

[5-8 HASHTAGS]
```

## Thread Formatting Rules

1. **Tweet 1 is everything.** Write it last, after you know the full thread. Spend 50% of your writing time on tweet 1.
2. **One idea per tweet.** If you are making two points in one tweet, split it.
3. **End strong tweets with a period.** Not an ellipsis. Not a question mark (unless it is a genuine question). Periods signal confidence.
4. **No hashtags until the final tweet.** This is non-negotiable.
5. **Number format: "1/" not "(1)" or "Tweet 1:"**. The slash format is standard and compact.
6. **Read each tweet out of context.** If it does not make sense alone, rewrite it.
7. **"A thread:" or "Thread:" on tweet 1.** Signals to readers that more is coming. Place it on a new line after the hook.
8. **Final tweet has 3 elements:** Takeaway or lesson, CTA (follow/RT/reply), and 5-8 hashtags.
