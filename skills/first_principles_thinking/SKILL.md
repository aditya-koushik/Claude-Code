# First Principles Thinking

## Purpose
Deconstruct any problem down to its fundamental truths by systematically identifying and challenging assumptions. Rebuilds solutions from the ground up using only verified axioms from physics, economics, mathematics, and human nature. Used by Karna and Duryodhana when conventional approaches are failing, costs seem immovable, or the team is trapped by "the way things have always been done."

## When to Use
- An industry convention is being accepted without question
- Cost structures seem fixed but feel too high
- Timeline estimates keep inflating with no clear reason
- The team says "that's impossible" without rigorous proof
- Evaluating whether a disruptive approach could work
- Creating content that challenges conventional business wisdom

## Workflow

### Step 1: State the Problem
Write the problem in a single, specific sentence. Include the current approach and its limitations. Define what success looks like without referencing how it is currently done.

### Step 2: List All Assumptions
Extract every assumption embedded in the current approach. Include explicit assumptions (stated by the team) and implicit assumptions (embedded in the process, pricing, timeline, or technology choice). Aim for at least 10 assumptions.

### Step 3: Challenge Each Assumption
For every assumption, ask: "Is this a law of physics or a convention of the industry?" Categorize each as:
- **Fundamental Truth**: Backed by physics, mathematics, or proven economics (keep)
- **Strong Convention**: Widely followed but not physically required (challenge)
- **Weak Assumption**: Based on habit, tradition, or limited data (discard)
Document the evidence for each categorization.

### Step 4: Identify Fundamental Truths
Distill the verified truths that remain after stripping assumptions. These are the bedrock: things that are true regardless of industry convention, company history, or current technology. Typically 3-5 truths remain.

### Step 5: Rebuild from Fundamentals
Using only the verified truths as building blocks, construct a new solution. Ignore the original approach entirely. Ask: "If I were starting from scratch with only these truths, what would I build?"

### Step 6: Compare with Conventional Approach
Place the rebuilt solution next to the conventional approach. Quantify the differences in cost, time, complexity, and effectiveness. Identify which differences are achievable today versus requiring future capability.

## Output Format
- **Problem Statement**: Single sentence
- **Assumption Inventory**: Numbered list with categorization (Truth/Convention/Assumption)
- **Fundamental Truths**: The 3-5 verified axioms that remain
- **Rebuilt Solution**: The ground-up approach
- **Comparison Table**: Conventional vs First Principles on cost, time, complexity, effectiveness
- **Implementation Gap**: What is needed to move from conventional to first-principles solution

## Key Principles
- Question everything "everyone knows"; most industry wisdom is just long-standing convention
- Cost structures can usually be reduced 10x when rebuilt from fundamentals
- Time estimates are usually 3x too high because they include assumption-driven buffers
- Most rules are conventions, not laws; only physics and mathematics are non-negotiable
- The biggest breakthroughs come from challenging the assumptions nobody thinks to question
