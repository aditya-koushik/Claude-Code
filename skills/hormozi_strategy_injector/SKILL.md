# Hormozi Strategy Injector

## Purpose
Apply Alex Hormozi's battle-tested business frameworks to any business decision, strategy, or content piece. Acts as a Hormozi lens that evaluates and upgrades any input through the Value Equation, Grand Slam Offers, Core Four marketing channels, and $100M Leads/Offers principles. Used by Drona to inject proven revenue-generation thinking into every strategic conversation and content output at Bit Foundry AI.

## When to Use
- Reviewing any business decision before execution
- Evaluating whether a content piece has enough value-driven punch
- Auditing current marketing strategy against Core Four channels
- Assessing an offer's strength before launch
- Upgrading a "good enough" strategy to a "prints money" strategy
- Writing content that teaches Hormozi frameworks to the audience

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify Applicable Framework
Determine which Hormozi framework(s) apply to the input:
- **Value Equation**: For pricing, offer design, and value communication
- **Grand Slam Offers**: For product/service packaging and bundling
- **Core Four**: For marketing channel strategy (Warm Outreach, Cold Outreach, Content, Paid Ads)
- **Lead Magnets**: For top-of-funnel and list building
- **$100M Leads**: For lead generation systems and volume
- **$100M Offers**: For offer architecture and market positioning

### Step 2: Apply Value Equation
Score the current state: Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice). Rate each variable 1-10. Identify the weakest variable and generate three specific improvements.

### Step 3: Audit Against Core Four
Evaluate current marketing efforts across all four channels:
- **Warm Outreach**: Are you reaching everyone who already knows you?
- **Cold Outreach**: Are you reaching 100+ new prospects daily?
- **Content**: Are you publishing value-first content daily on key platforms?
- **Paid Ads**: Are you scaling what works with paid amplification?
Identify which channels are underutilized or missing entirely.

### Step 4: Assess Offer Quality
Rate the current offer against Grand Slam criteria: Does it solve the dream outcome? Does it stack enough value? Is there a risk-reversing guarantee? Is there real scarcity? Would a rational person feel stupid saying no?

### Step 5: Recommend Improvements
Produce specific, actionable improvements using the $100M framework. Prioritize by impact-to-effort ratio. Every recommendation must include: what to change, why (which framework principle), and expected impact.

## Output Format
- **Framework Match**: Which Hormozi frameworks apply and why
- **Value Equation Score**: Current score with variable breakdown (1-10 each)
- **Core Four Audit**: Channel-by-channel assessment with gaps highlighted
- **Offer Assessment**: Grand Slam score with specific weaknesses
- **Top 5 Improvements**: Prioritized actions with framework rationale and expected impact
- **Hormozi Quotable**: One relevant Hormozi principle stated as a memorable one-liner

## Key Principles
- 100 leads per day is the minimum viable pipeline; below that, nothing else matters
- Underpriced equals undervalued; charge what the transformation is worth
- Give away the recipe, sell the restaurant; free content builds trust, paid offers deliver transformation
- Volume negates luck; do enough outreach and statistics work in your favor
- The best offer in the world fails without distribution; the best distribution fails without a good offer
