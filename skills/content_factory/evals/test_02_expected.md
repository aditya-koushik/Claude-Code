# Expected Output Characteristics: Test 02 (Edge Case)

## Pipeline Execution Must Exhibit

1. **Source extraction stage:** Agastya (or equivalent) extracts key insights from the transcript before writing begins. Key extraction: "trust through transparency", "audit trails", "kill switch", "no black boxes".
2. **Research supplement:** Even though source material is provided, the pipeline should verify/supplement with external data (e.g., market data on AI trust, examples of AI failures due to lack of transparency).
3. **Content framed as thought leadership, NOT product pitch:** The output should teach CTOs what to demand from AI vendors. Bit Foundry mentioned minimally or not at all. The audience learns something valuable regardless of who made the content.
4. **Dual platform output:** Both LinkedIn and Twitter versions produced.
5. **Quality gate applied with transcript verification:** Reviewer should verify that quotes/paraphrases from the transcript are accurate. No fabricated quotes attributed to the CEO/CTO.
6. **Visual asset produced.**
7. **Saved as DRAFT only.**

## Content Quality Must Exhibit

8. **"Trust through transparency" as the core thesis:** This is the headline the CEO explicitly requested.
9. **Audience-centric framing:** Written TO CTOs about what THEY should demand. Not "we built this" but "here's what to look for."
10. **Specific actionable advice:** Must include concrete things CTOs should demand (audit trails, reasoning chains, kill switches, no black boxes) — derived from the transcript but framed as industry advice.
11. **No direct quotes from the internal call:** The transcript is internal. Output should synthesize insights, not quote the CEO/CTO conversation directly.
12. **Bit Foundry AI voice:** Direct, enterprise lens, no hedging.
13. **Zero banned words.**

## Must NOT Exhibit

1. Direct quotes from the CEO/CTO conversation in the published content (this is internal)
2. Product pitch framing ("We built...", "Our platform...", "Bit Foundry offers...")
3. Writing that reads as a press release or announcement
4. Missing research supplement (relying solely on transcript)
5. Only one platform output
6. Auto-publishing
