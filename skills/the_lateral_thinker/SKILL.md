# The Lateral Thinker

## Purpose
Phase 3 of the 6-phase content enhancement pipeline. Answers the question: "What's the deeper pattern? The analogy?" Takes fact-checked content and enriches it with cross-domain analogies, historical parallels, and unexpected connections that make the content memorable and shareable. This is where content goes from accurate to insightful.

## When to Use
- Content has passed fact-checking (Phase 2) and needs depth
- A technical concept needs a relatable analogy for a business audience
- Content feels correct but flat and forgettable
- An argument needs a historical precedent to strengthen it

## Workflow
### Step 1: Identify Core Concept
- Distill the central idea into a single abstract principle
- Example: "AI replacing jobs" becomes "new technology displacing incumbent labor"

### Step 2: Search for Cross-Domain Analogies
- Scan history, science, sports, warfare, economics, nature for parallel patterns
- Look for situations where the same abstract principle played out in a different domain
- Prioritize analogies the audience will recognize but not expect

### Step 3: Find Historical Parallels
- Identify specific historical events that mirror the current situation
- Note how those situations resolved (for predictive insight)
- Include the timeline and key actors for credibility

### Step 4: Generate "What If" Perspectives
- Invert assumptions: what if the opposite were true?
- Shift viewpoint: how does this look from the competitor, the customer, the regulator?
- Extend the timeline: what does this mean in 5 years, 10 years, 50 years?

### Step 5: Select and Refine
- Choose the 2-3 strongest analogies (clarity + surprise + relevance)
- Write each as a tight paragraph that connects back to the core argument
- Discard any analogy that requires extensive explanation to work

## Output Format
- **Analogies:** 2-3 polished analogies with source domain and connection explained
- **Historical Parallel:** One detailed parallel with timeline and resolution
- **Perspective Shift:** One "what if" reframe that challenges the obvious take
- **Phase 3 Status:** ENRICHED (strong analogies found) or STANDARD (proceed without, topic is too narrow)

## Key Principles
- Cross-domain thinking: the best analogies come from unrelated fields
- Surprise the reader; if the analogy is obvious, it adds no value
- Analogies must illuminate, not confuse; if it needs a footnote, cut it
- One great analogy beats three mediocre ones
- The audience is enterprise leaders; analogies from business, history, and strategy land hardest
