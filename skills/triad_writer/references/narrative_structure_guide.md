# Narrative Structure Guide

## How to Blend Archetypes Within a Single Piece

Pure archetypes feel one-dimensional. The best long-form content leads with one archetype and blends in 1-2 others for depth. This guide shows when to lead with each archetype and how to mix them.

---

## When to Lead with Each Archetype

### Lead with HERO When:

**Content goals:**
- Launching a product or feature
- Making a bold claim about what you built
- Sharing a customer transformation story
- Telling a pivot, failure, or comeback story
- Building emotional connection with the audience

**The Hero arc:**
1. **Setup** - The problem. The pain. The moment before change.
2. **Struggle** - What you tried. What failed. The low point.
3. **Transformation** - What changed. The breakthrough.
4. **Lesson** - What the reader takes away.

**When Hero feels flat, add:**
- **Sage elements:** After the transformation, teach a framework from the experience. "Here are the 3 rules I now follow because of this."
- **Sovereign elements:** At the end, make a bold claim about what this means for the industry. "This is the future of how products get built."

**Example blend - Hero + Sage:**
> [HERO] I launched our AI agent and it hallucinated pricing data on day one. I pulled the feature. Spent 3 weeks rebuilding with a validation layer. Relaunched. Zero hallucinations in 90 days.
>
> [SAGE] Here is the framework that saved us: Test on real data, not clean data. Validate every output against the source system. Deploy to 10 users before 10,000.

---

### Lead with SAGE When:

**Content goals:**
- Teaching a concept, framework, or process
- Sharing lessons from experience
- Creating educational content (how-to, tutorial, breakdown)
- Establishing expertise on a specific topic
- Answering a question your audience frequently asks

**The Sage arc:**
1. **Question** - What people get wrong. The misconception.
2. **Framework** - The better way. Your model, rules, or system.
3. **Evidence** - Proof it works. Data, examples, case studies.
4. **Application** - How to start. The first step.

**When Sage feels dry, add:**
- **Hero elements:** Open with the personal story of how you learned this lesson. "I burned $200K before I figured this out."
- **Sovereign elements:** Close with a prediction about what happens to people who ignore this framework. "Companies that skip this step will spend 3x more to catch up in 2027."

**Example blend - Sage + Hero:**
> [HERO] I priced our product at $49/month for 14 months. Revenue never broke $50K MRR. I was afraid to charge more.
>
> [SAGE] Here is what I learned about enterprise pricing. Price on value, not cost. If your tool saves them $500K per year, $50K per year is a bargain. Raise prices every 6 months. Grandfather existing customers. If close rates stay above 15%, you are still too cheap.

---

### Lead with SOVEREIGN When:

**Content goals:**
- Making an industry prediction
- Taking a strong position on a debate
- Analyzing a market shift or trend
- Positioning yourself as a category authority
- Responding to major industry news

**The Sovereign arc:**
1. **Observation** - What is happening in the market. The trend.
2. **Position** - Your take. What it means. Why it matters.
3. **Vision** - Where this leads in 1-5 years.
4. **Call** - What to do about it. The challenge.

**When Sovereign feels arrogant, add:**
- **Sage evidence:** Back every bold claim with specific data. "This is not my opinion. McKinsey surveyed 1,400 companies and found the same pattern."
- **Hero vulnerability:** Admit a time you got a prediction wrong, then explain why this time is different. "I thought SaaS bundling would last another decade. I was wrong. Here is what changed my mind."

**Example blend - Sovereign + Sage:**
> [SOVEREIGN] The era of the all-in-one SaaS platform is ending. AI agents make best-of-breed integration trivial. The lock-in advantage disappears.
>
> [SAGE] Here is how to position for this shift. First, audit your tool stack. Which tools would you keep if switching costs dropped to zero? Second, invest in API-first architecture. Third, build workflow automation that connects your best tools instead of replacing them with one mediocre platform.

## Blending Matrix

| If the piece feels... | Add this archetype | How |
|----------------------|-------------------|-----|
| Self-centered (too much "I") | Sage | Add a framework the reader can use |
| Dry and academic | Hero | Add a personal story that illustrates the point |
| Arrogant or preachy | Sage with data | Back every claim with specific evidence |
| Abstract and theoretical | Hero | Ground the theory in a real example |
| Too personal, not authoritative | Sovereign | Add a market prediction or industry position |
| Informational but boring | Hero + Sovereign | Open with a story, close with a bold vision |

## Archetype Transition Phrases

When blending archetypes, these transition patterns help shift the energy:

**Hero to Sage:**
- "Here is what I learned from that."
- "That experience taught me three rules."
- "The framework that saved us:"

**Hero to Sovereign:**
- "This is not just my story. This is the future."
- "What happened to us will happen to every company that..."
- "This pattern is bigger than one company."

**Sage to Hero:**
- "I did not learn this from a textbook."
- "This framework came from a failure."
- "Here is the story behind rule #1."

**Sage to Sovereign:**
- "Companies that ignore this will..."
- "The market is moving toward..."
- "In 18 months, this will not be optional."

**Sovereign to Sage:**
- "Here is how to position for this shift."
- "Three things you can do about it today."
- "The framework for navigating this change:"

**Sovereign to Hero:**
- "I saw this firsthand."
- "We experienced this shift before most."
- "Let me tell you what happened when we ignored this trend."

## Archetype Selection Quick Reference

| Content Trigger | Lead Archetype | Why |
|----------------|---------------|-----|
| "We just launched..." | Hero | People want the story behind the product |
| "How to..." | Sage | Readers want a framework to follow |
| "Breaking: [industry news]" | Sovereign | Take a position before everyone else does |
| "I failed at..." | Hero | Vulnerability builds trust |
| "The 3 rules of..." | Sage | Teach from earned authority |
| "By 2028..." | Sovereign | Predictions position you as a visionary |
| "Customer case study" | Hero (customer as hero) | Let the customer's journey sell the product |
| "Framework breakdown" | Sage | Pure teaching mode |
| "The market is wrong about..." | Sovereign | Contrarian takes demand authority |
| "We grew from X to Y" | Hero + Sage | Tell the story, then teach the framework |

## Rules for Blending

1. **One dominant archetype per piece.** Do not split 50/50. Go 70/30 or 80/20.
2. **The dominant archetype sets the opening and closing.** Secondary archetypes appear in the middle.
3. **Hero opens with a moment. Sage opens with a question. Sovereign opens with a declaration.** Match the opening to the dominant archetype.
4. **Never blend all three equally.** A piece that tries to be a story, a lesson, and a vision statement at the same time is a mess.
5. **The blend should feel natural.** If you have to force a transition between archetypes, the blend is wrong. Pick a different combination.
