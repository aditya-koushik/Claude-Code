# LinkedIn Algorithm Best Practices 2026

## How the Algorithm Works in 2026

LinkedIn's feed algorithm scores every post on three axes: relevance, engagement velocity, and creator authority. Posts that score high on all three get shown to exponentially more people. Posts that miss on any one axis get buried.

---

## Post Length

**Optimal range: 150-300 words.**

- Posts under 100 words get lower distribution. The algorithm reads short posts as low-effort.
- Posts between 150-300 words hit the sweet spot. Long enough to deliver value. Short enough that mobile users finish reading.
- Posts over 500 words see a distribution drop unless engagement is exceptional. Long posts work for established creators with high comment rates. Not for growing accounts.
- The "See more" fold sits at roughly 210 characters (about 3 lines on mobile). Everything above the fold determines whether someone clicks to expand. Treat the first 210 characters as your ad for the rest of the post.

---

## Engagement Triggers

The algorithm weights engagement types differently:

| Action | Algorithm Weight | Why |
|--------|-----------------|-----|
| Comment (10+ words) | Highest | Signals real conversation |
| Share with commentary | High | Extends reach to new networks |
| Save/Bookmark | High | Signals lasting value |
| Reaction (any type) | Medium | Quick signal but low effort |
| Click "See more" | Medium | Proves the hook worked |
| Share without commentary | Low | Often automated or accidental |
| Profile view from post | Medium-High | Signals curiosity about the author |

**Key insight:** One thoughtful comment is worth more than 50 likes. Write posts that provoke responses, not agreement.

**Engagement velocity matters:** A post that gets 10 comments in the first 30 minutes outperforms a post that gets 50 comments over 24 hours. The algorithm measures how fast engagement accumulates, not just how much.

## Hashtag Strategy

**Use exactly 8-10 hashtags. Space-separated. No commas.**

### Rules:
- Place hashtags on the very last line of the post. Never inside the body.
- Mix broad and niche:
  - 3-4 broad hashtags (500K+ followers): #AI #Leadership #SaaS #Entrepreneurship
  - 3-4 mid-range (50K-500K followers): #EnterpriseAI #B2BSaaS #AIStrategy #TechLeadership
  - 2-3 niche (under 50K followers): #BuildInPublic #AIForBusiness #SaaSFounder
- No commas between hashtags. Space-separated only.
- Do not use hashtags that have nothing to do with the post. The algorithm penalizes irrelevant hashtag use.
- Rotate hashtags across posts. Using the same 10 hashtags on every post triggers spam detection.

## Hook Patterns That Win

The first 2 lines determine 80% of engagement. The hook must stop the scroll.

**Top-performing hook types in 2026:**

1. **Contrarian:** Challenge a popular belief. "Everyone says AI will replace developers. They are wrong."
2. **Specific number:** Lead with a result. "We cut our sales cycle from 47 days to 12."
3. **Bold claim:** Make a declarative statement. "The system of record is dead."
4. **Question:** Ask something the reader cannot ignore. "What happens when your SaaS vendor disappears overnight?"
5. **Story opener:** Drop into a specific moment. "Last Tuesday, our biggest client called and said three words."
6. **Pattern interrupt:** Say something unexpected. "I fired our best-performing sales rep."
7. **List promise:** Tell them what they will get. "7 things I learned after selling to 200 enterprises."

**Hook killers (avoid these):**
- Starting with "I am excited to announce..."
- Starting with "In today's rapidly evolving..."
- Starting with a hashtag
- Starting with a generic greeting
- Starting with "Happy to share..."
- Any opener that sounds like a press release

## Posting Times (Optimal Windows)

**Best performing time slots (UTC):**

| Day | Best Times (UTC) | Why |
|-----|-----------------|-----|
| Tuesday | 7:00-8:30 AM | Professionals check LinkedIn before first meeting |
| Wednesday | 7:00-8:30 AM, 12:00-1:00 PM | Morning scroll + lunch break |
| Thursday | 7:00-8:30 AM | Strong engagement before weekend wind-down starts |
| Monday | 8:00-9:00 AM | Week kickoff, but more noise from corporate posts |
| Friday | 7:00-8:00 AM | Lower volume = less competition, but lower total engagement |
| Saturday/Sunday | Skip | 40-60% lower engagement for B2B content |

**Key insight:** Post once per business day. Posting twice in one day cannibalizes your own reach. The algorithm gives each post roughly 24 hours of distribution. If you post again before the first post peaks, you split your audience.

## Content Format Performance (2026 Rankings)

| Format | Avg. Reach | Avg. Engagement | Best For |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| Carousel (PDF document) | Highest | High | Frameworks, step-by-step guides, lists |
| Text + single image | High | Highest | Hot takes, stories, insights with supporting visual |
| Text only | Medium-High | High | Contrarian takes, short stories, questions |
| Video (native upload) | Medium | Medium | Personal stories, behind-the-scenes |
| Poll | Medium-High | Medium (shallow) | Audience research, engagement farming |
| Article (LinkedIn native) | Low | Low | Do not use. Posts outperform articles 5-to-1 |
| External link | Lowest | Lowest | Algorithm actively suppresses outbound links |

**Key insight:** If you must share an external link, put it in the first comment, not in the post body. Posts with external links in the body get 40-50% less reach.

## Algorithm Signals to Maximize

1. **Dwell time:** Write content that takes 30+ seconds to read. The algorithm tracks how long people stay on your post.
2. **Comment depth:** Reply to every comment on your post within the first 2 hours. This creates comment threads, which the algorithm treats as high-quality engagement.
3. **Creator consistency:** Post at least 3x per week. The algorithm rewards consistent creators with higher baseline distribution.
4. **Network relevance:** Your post first shows to your strongest connections. If they engage, it expands. Build real connections with people in your target audience.
5. **Profile completeness:** Profiles with headline, about section, featured section, and 500+ connections get a distribution boost.

---

## Things That Kill Reach

- Editing a post within the first hour (resets distribution)
- Tagging more than 5 people (spam signal)
- Using engagement pods (LinkedIn detects and penalizes)
- Posting external links in the body
- Using the same hashtags on every post
- Posting more than once per day
- Deleting and reposting (kills all accumulated signals)
