# Post Structure Template: The 1/3/1 Rhythm

## What Is 1/3/1?

The 1/3/1 sentence rhythm is the backbone of every high-performing LinkedIn post. It means:

- **1** sentence (hook, standalone)
- **3** sentences (body paragraph, one idea developed)
- **1** sentence (transition, insight, or punch)

Repeat the 3/1 pattern 2-3 times, then close with a CTA and hashtags.

This rhythm creates natural pacing. The single-sentence lines act as breathing room. The 3-sentence blocks deliver substance. The alternation keeps readers moving down the page.

---

## The Full Structure

```
[1] HOOK - One sentence. Standalone. Stops the scroll.

[3] CONTEXT - Three sentences. Set up the situation, problem, or background.

[1] PIVOT - One sentence. The "but" or "here is the thing" moment.

[3] INSIGHT - Three sentences. Your main point, framework, or lesson.

[1] PUNCH - One sentence. The takeaway. Bold. Memorable.

[1] CTA - One sentence. Question or invitation.

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3 #Hashtag4 #Hashtag5 #Hashtag6 #Hashtag7 #Hashtag8
```

## Annotated Example 1: Contrarian Take (AI Implementation)

```
Most AI projects fail because companies buy tools before defining problems.

I have watched 40+ enterprises attempt AI implementation this year.
The pattern is the same every time. Executive reads an article,
calls a meeting, and says "We need AI."

That is not a strategy. That is a shopping spree.

The companies that succeed do three things differently.
They start with a specific workflow that costs them real money.
They measure baseline performance before writing a single line of code.

The tool is the last decision. Not the first.

What is the most expensive manual process in your business right now?

#AI #EnterpriseAI #AIStrategy #SaaS #DigitalTransformation #Leadership #BuildInPublic #AIImplementation
```

**Word count:** 118 words

**Breakdown:**
- [1] HOOK: "Most AI projects fail because companies buy tools before defining problems." Contrarian. Specific. Stops the scroll.
- [3] CONTEXT: Three sentences establishing the pattern from direct observation. "40+ enterprises" adds credibility.
- [1] PIVOT: "That is not a strategy. That is a shopping spree." Two short fragments that reframe the behavior as obviously wrong.
- [3] INSIGHT: Three sentences delivering the framework. Each sentence is one specific action.
- [1] PUNCH: "The tool is the last decision. Not the first." Memorable. Quotable.
- [1] CTA: A question that invites comments by asking about personal experience.
- HASHTAGS: 8 hashtags. Space-separated. Mix of broad (#AI, #SaaS) and niche (#BuildInPublic, #AIImplementation).

**Word count:** 115 words

**Breakdown:**

## Annotated Example 3: Teaching Framework (Content Strategy)

```
Your content team produces 10x more with AI. Quality stays the same.

Most content teams use AI wrong.
They paste a topic into ChatGPT, get a draft, and publish it.
Then they wonder why engagement dropped 40%.

AI is an accelerator. Not a replacement.

Here is the framework that works.
Step 1: Human writes the outline and angle.
Step 2: AI generates 3 draft variations. Step 3: Human edits the best one, adds voice, cuts the fluff.

The human does 20% of the work. But that 20% is what readers care about.

How does your team use AI in the content workflow?

#AI #ContentMarketing #ContentStrategy #SaaS #MarketingOps #GenerativeAI #BuildInPublic #AIForBusiness
```

**Word count:** 127 words

**Breakdown:**
- [1] HOOK: Bold promise with a qualifier. 10x output AND quality holds. That combination demands attention.
- [3] CONTEXT: Identifies the common mistake with a specific example (paste into ChatGPT).
- [1] PIVOT: Redefines AI's role in one line. Accelerator vs replacement.
- [3] INSIGHT: Numbered steps. Concrete. Actionable. Each step is one sentence.
- [1] PUNCH: Surprising ratio (20%) that makes the point memorable.
- [1] CTA: Invites sharing of personal workflow. Drives comments.
- HASHTAGS: 8 hashtags mixing content marketing and AI audiences.

## Formatting Rules

1. **Blank line between every element.** Every single-sentence line and every paragraph gets a blank line before and after it. White space is what makes this work on mobile.

2. **No paragraph longer than 3 sentences.** If you hit 4 sentences, split the paragraph or cut a sentence.

3. **Bold 2-4 key phrases.** Bold the framework name, the surprising number, or the main takeaway. Do not bold entire sentences.

4. **Hook line is always alone.** No second sentence on the same line as the hook.

5. **CTA is always a question.** Questions drive comments. Comments drive reach. "What is your take?" is acceptable but "Have you experienced this?" is better because it asks for a story.

6. **Hashtags on the final line.** 8-10 hashtags. Space-separated. No commas. No hashtags anywhere else in the post.

7. **Target 150-300 words total.** Count words. If you are over 300, cut. If you are under 150, develop one section further.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Hook is 2 sentences | Cut to 1. Always. |
| Body paragraphs are 5+ sentences | Split into two paragraphs |
| No blank lines between paragraphs | Add a blank line after every paragraph |
| CTA says "Like and share" | Ask a real question instead |
| Hashtags scattered through the post | Move all hashtags to the last line |
| Post is 400+ words | Cut 30%. Be ruthless. |
| Every paragraph is the same length | Vary between 1 and 3 sentences |
| Bold used on 8+ phrases | Limit to 2-4 phrases max |
