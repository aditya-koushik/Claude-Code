## Raw Content

The system of record is dying. Most enterprise leaders don't see it yet, but AI is making the entire concept of a "single source of truth" database obsolete. Here's why. Traditional SaaS tools store your data in their silo. Salesforce owns your CRM data. HubSpot owns your marketing data. Jira owns your project data. You pay four vendors to store four copies of information that should live in one place. AI changes this equation completely. An orchestration layer sits above all your tools and treats them as interchangeable components. The data flows through the layer, not into the tool. The tool becomes a feature, not a platform. Infosys figured this out early. They consolidated 47 SaaS tools down to 12 in Q4 2025. Their integration maintenance costs dropped 63%. The engineers who used to maintain those integrations now build product. The companies that understand this shift will own the next decade of enterprise software. The ones that don't will keep paying the SaaS tax until their competitors make it irrelevant. The question isn't whether this shift is coming. It's whether you'll be ready when it arrives.
