# Expected Output Characteristics: Test 02 (Edge Case)

## Must Exhibit

1. **Word count between 150-300 words:** The 450+ word input MUST be trimmed. Output cannot exceed 300 words (excluding hashtags).
2. **Key data points preserved despite trimming:** Must retain at least 3 of these: "40 engineering hours/month", "$150,000", "94% drop in integration failures", "40 hours to 3 hours", "O(n-squared) to O(n)". The most impactful numbers survive the cut.
3. **Core message intact:** The "Integration Tax" concept and orchestration layer solution must remain. The thesis cannot be lost in trimming.
4. **Hook on line 1:** A scroll-stopping opener. Could be "Your engineers are babysitting APIs instead of building product." or the Integration Tax framing.
5. **8-10 hashtags on final line:** Space-separated, no commas.
6. **Mobile formatting maintained:** Even after trimming, paragraphs stay 1-3 sentences with blank lines.
7. **CTA present:** Trimming does not remove the call-to-action.
8. **2-4 bolded phrases:** Key stats or concepts bolded.

## Must NOT Exhibit

1. Word count above 300 (the main test of this edge case)
2. Loss of all supporting data (kept at least 3 key numbers)
3. The trimmed version reads as choppy or incoherent — cuts must be clean
4. Important context removed while filler retained (should cut filler first, preserve proof)
5. No hashtags with commas
6. No paragraphs > 3 sentences
7. The three closing questions do NOT need to survive trimming — they are lower priority than the proof points
