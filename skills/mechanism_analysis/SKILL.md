# Mechanism Analysis

## Purpose
Analyze how systems, markets, and organizations actually work beneath the surface by mapping inputs, outputs, causal chains, feedback loops, and leverage points. Strips away narratives and marketing to reveal the real mechanism driving outcomes. Used by Karna and Duryodhana when the team needs to understand why something works (or doesn't), not just that it works.

## When to Use
- A business model or growth tactic is working but nobody understands why
- Evaluating a competitor's strategy by understanding their actual mechanism
- Diagnosing why a strategy that should work is underperforming
- Creating content that explains "how things really work" to the audience
- Identifying leverage points where small changes produce outsized results

## Workflow

### Step 1: Identify the System
Define the boundaries of the system being analyzed. Name it clearly: "SaaS freemium conversion engine" not "our business." Specify what is inside the system (controllable) and what is environment (context).

### Step 2: Map Inputs and Outputs
List every input that enters the system (traffic, money, time, data, labor) and every output it produces (revenue, users, content, reputation, data). Quantify where possible. Identify which outputs are desired and which are waste or byproducts.

### Step 3: Trace the Causal Chain
Walk through the system step by step: Input A causes Process B, which produces Output C, which feeds back into Process D. Document every link in the chain. Mark which links are strong (proven, reliable) and which are weak (assumed, untested).

### Step 4: Identify Feedback Loops
Find the reinforcing loops (virtuous cycles that accelerate growth) and balancing loops (diminishing returns, natural ceilings). Examples: network effects (reinforcing), market saturation (balancing), brand reputation (reinforcing), team burnout (balancing).

### Step 5: Find Leverage Points
Identify the 2-3 points in the system where a small change produces a disproportionately large effect. These are typically: bottleneck steps, feedback loop triggers, or information flow nodes. Rank leverage points by impact and accessibility.

### Step 6: Explain in Simple Terms
Produce a plain-language explanation of the mechanism that a non-expert could understand. Use analogies where helpful. The explanation should make the reader say "now I understand why that works."

## Output Format
- **System Definition**: Name, boundaries, and scope
- **Input-Output Map**: All inputs and outputs with quantities where available
- **Causal Chain Diagram**: Step-by-step flow described textually (A causes B causes C)
- **Feedback Loops**: Reinforcing and balancing loops identified and named
- **Leverage Points**: Top 2-3 with impact rating and recommended action
- **Plain-Language Explanation**: The mechanism in 3-5 sentences a non-expert understands
- **Second-Order Effects**: Non-obvious downstream consequences of the mechanism

## Key Principles
- Follow the money to understand incentives; incentives explain 90% of behavior
- Most complex systems have 2-3 key variables; everything else is noise
- Second-order effects matter more than first-order; always ask "and then what?"
- The map is not the territory; mechanisms on paper differ from mechanisms in practice
- If you cannot explain the mechanism simply, you do not understand it yet
