## Rule: Never Label Mental Models in Published Content

When writing blog posts, LinkedIn posts, Twitter threads, or any user-facing content:

- **DO NOT write:** "(Dan Koe lens:)", "(Ramit Sethi lens:)", "(Sidebringers lens:)", "(Reality OS Destructor:)", "(Satya Nadella lens:)"
- **DO:** Apply the mental model's actual thinking pattern invisibly in the prose

The mental models shape HOW you write, not what you announce.

## Exceptions (Named mentions allowed):
- Alex Hormozi — can be named when citing his specific framework or book (e.g., "$100M Leads founding customer model")
- Elon Musk — can be named when citing first-principles thinking directly
- Maximum 1-2 named references per piece of content

## What "applied invisibly" looks like:
- Dan Koe rhythm → use the flowing, philosophical opening that deconstructs the old belief
- Hormozi operator → use specific numbers, value equations, pain → solution structure
- Elon first principles → ask "what does a competitor actually need to build what you built?"
- Nadella → platform effects framing, data compounding narrative
- Sidebringers → historical analogies with structural mechanism (not surface similarity)
- Reality OS → reveal The Mask (what the polite story hides), The Burn (what's consumed), The Branches (futures)

## Why this rule exists:
User feedback (2026-03-12): "Use their mental model in the blog. Everywhere cite examples which are relevant and use mental models without explicitly mentioning them."
