## Rule: Eliminate Ultra-Short Sentence Fragment Patterns

**Problem:** AI-generated content frequently uses clusters of very short sentences (2-4 words each) ending in full stops as a dramatic or rhythmic device. This is an obvious AI writing fingerprint that humans rarely produce.

**Examples of what NOT to do:**
- "The plumbing. The fixtures. The harness."
- "The customer never knows. They do not need to."
- "Not the model. The orchestration."
- Any pattern of 2-3 consecutive sentences where each has fewer than 5 words

**What to do instead:**

1. **Use medium sentences (10-15 words):** Combine the short fragments into a single flowing sentence that reads naturally.
   - BAD: "The model is the commodity. The harness is the moat."
   - GOOD: "The model has become a commodity, while the harness remains the true moat."

2. **Use bullet lists with "-" for enumeration:** If you need to list short items, use a bulleted list format instead of consecutive full-stop sentences.
   - BAD: "The plumbing. The fixtures. The harness."
   - GOOD:
     - The plumbing
     - The fixtures
     - The harness

3. **Minimum sentence length guidance:**
   - Short sentences: 5-9 words (use sparingly, never consecutively)
   - Medium sentences: 10-15 words (default target)
   - Long sentences: 16-25 words (use for complex ideas)
   - Never write consecutive sentences with fewer than 5 words each

**Applies to:** All content formats (blog, LinkedIn, Twitter, email, scripts)
**Reason:** Primary AI-writing fingerprint. Kills authenticity. Humans naturally write in medium-length sentences.
